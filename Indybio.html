PO5

Patricio O'Ward

Pato O'Ward is a Mexican racing driver who currently competes in the IndyCar Series for Arrow McLaren in the No. 5 Chevrolet. He was born in Monterrey, Mexico, on May 6, 1999. He started off in karting and progressing on to Formula Renault 1.6 NEC and Pacific Formula F2000 in 2013. In 2014, he also competed in the French F4 Championship before moving on to Indy Lights and the Pro Mazda Championship. In the PC class, he won the 2018 Indy Lights championship and the 2017 IMSA WeatherTech SportsCar Championship. He made his IndyCar debut in 2018 with Harding Racing and won his first race at Texas Motor Speedway in 2021. He won the award of Rookie of the Year for the 2020 Indianapolis 500.

FR6

Felix Rosenqvist

Felix Rosenqvist is a Swedish professional racing driver who currently is competing for Arrow McLaren in the NTT IndyCar Series in the No. 6 Chevrolet. He was named IndyCar Series Rookie of the Year in 2019 and won a race in Elkhart Lake, Wis., in 2020. He competed in Formula 3 and Formula E before joining IndyCar, winning the 2015 FIA Formula 3 European Championship and three Formula E races. He is also the winner of the Masters of F3 and the Macau Grand Prix twice.

AR7

Alexander Rossi

Alexander Rossi is an American racing driver and podcast broadcaster who races with Arrow McLaren in the IndyCar Series in the No. 7 Chevrolet. He began his career in karting and went on to win the Formula BMW World Championship in 2008. In 2015, he raced for the Manor Marussia F1 Team after moving to Europe to pursue Formula One. In 2016, he returned to the United States and joined Andretti Autosport, where he won the Indianapolis 500 as a rookie. In 2018, he finished second in the drivers' championship and has eight IndyCar victories to his name.

TK66

Tony Kanaan

Tony Kanaan is a Brazilian racing driver who competes part-time in the IndyCar Series, driving the No. 66 Chevrolet for Arrow McLaren. He is the 2013 Indianapolis 500 winner and the 2004 IndyCar Series champion. He has 16 IndyCar wins and 11 poles in his career. He also competed in Champ Car from 1998 to 2002, winning one race and four poles. He began his racing career in karting and Formula Europa Boxer in Brazil before moving to Europe and the U.S. to pursue higher levels of motorsport. He is known for his popularity among fans and his endurance on ovals, having completed every lap of every race in the 2004 season. He also holds the record for most consecutive starts in IndyCar history with 318.
